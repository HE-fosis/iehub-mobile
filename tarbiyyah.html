Toolkit & FAQ's

WHAT IS RAMADAN?

Tap to flip

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar. It is a month of fasting and one of the five pillars of Islam. It is a time of spiritual growth, marking the month of the Qur'an's divine revelation.

WHAT HAPPENS DURING RAMADAN?

Tap to flip

We wake up before dawn for a meal called Suhoor and fast and abstain from impulses to focus on reflection. At sunset, we break it at Iftar with water and dates as that is the sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ .

IS IT JUST NO FOOD AND WATER?

Tap to flip

No, it is a spiritual reset. Beyond food and water, we work on controlling our desires and nafs (self) - avoiding gossip and anger to connect deeper with Allah (SWT).

WHAT IF WE EAT IN FRONT OF YOU?

Tap to flip

There is actually a special reward for us when others eat in our presence:

Umm 'Umarah bint Ka'b reported: The Prophet ﷺ said "Verily, angels send blessings upon a fasting person when others eat in their presence until they finish."
— Tirmidhi 785

WHY DO YOU FAST?

Tap to flip

Fasting is about obeying Allah's command and aiming to achieve Taqwa (God-consciousness). It builds self-discipline and spiritual growth. It is a purification of the soul through increased prayer.

HOW TO SUPPORT?

Tap to flip

A simple "Ramadan Mubarak" means a lot and everyone is welcome to join us for Iftar! Showing understanding, flexibility, and respect helps keep us motivated.

Virtues of Feeding People

“From the ‘ibadah of providing food for people stem many other acts of worship such as creating love and friendship towards those who are given the food, which is a means of entering Paradise, as the Prophet ﷺ said: “You will not enter Paradise until you truly believe, and you will not truly believe until you love one another.” (Muslim, 54). It also fosters the practice of sitting with righteous people and seeking reward by helping them to do acts of worship for which they gain strength by eating your food. And Allah knows best.”
Renew and Realign Intentions
Aligning the heart and mind is the foundation of service. Remind your team that providing food is a profound act of 'ibadah with immense spiritual weight.
“Whoever gives iftar to one who is fasting will have a reward like his, without that detracting from the reward of the fasting person in the slightest.” — Narrated by al-Tirmidhi, 807; Ibn Majah, 1746.
Pre-Iftar Logistics & Practical Essentials
  • Plan a menu: Accommodate for GF, Vegetarian, Vegan, and Nut-free options.
  • Waste Management: Set up a clear system for food waste and recycling.
  • Space: Dedicated spot for dates and water.
  • Prayer Space Prep: Use plastic sheets to transition quickly.
Budgeting & Resource Management
  • Track Expenses: Maintain a live spreadsheet.
  • Sponsorships: Partner with local businesses.
  • Bulk Purchasing: Buy dates and water in bulk.
Financial Inclusivity
University life can be financially difficult. By providing free iftars, you can support students under hardship and build a sanctuary where everyone is welcome regardless of their situation. Ensure no student is left behind.
“Whoever relieves a believer of some worldly distress, Allah will relieve him of some of the distress of the Day of Resurrection.” — Ibn Majah, 225
Dawah Outreach
The iftar table is a beautiful opportunity for outreach. Invite non-Muslims to experience the spirituality of eating and praying together, allowing them to witness the unity and hospitality of the Muslim community first-hand.
“And who is better in speech than one who invites to Allah...” — Quran 41:33
Fundraising & Charity Iftars
Dedicate specific nights to charity. Organize fundraising iftars or ticketed iftars to support local or global causes, strengthening the community’s commitment to giving.

Physical Benefits of Fasting

Metabolic Control

Fasting improves insulin sensitivity and helps regulate blood sugar levels.

Cellular Repair

Fasting triggers autophagy, where cells "clean house" and trigger regeneration.

Heart Health

Improves cholesterol levels and lowers blood pressure.

Inflammation

Decreases markers of chronic inflammation, the root cause of many diseases.

Cognitive

Boosts brain function and supports mental clarity and nerve cell growth.

Gut Health

Promotes hormonal balance and improves gut health through digestive rest.

The Internal Battle

Procrastination is often used as a maladaptive coping mechanism to avoid difficult feelings. It is the result of a structural conflict between the Limbic System (Emotional Hub) and the Prefrontal Cortex (Rational Planner).

The Shaytan's Tactic: Procrastination is viewed as a spiritual trial—a way to induce Ghafla (heedlessness) and detach us from our purpose through self-guilt.

Roots of Avoidance:

  • Tul al-Amal: The delusion that we have many years to live.
  • Perfectionism: The fear that "imperfect" work is a failure.
  • Fatigue: Being tired is the #1 biological accelerator of delay.

Reframing the Load

Overwhelming stress is often caused by trying to control an uncertain future. The solution is Tawakkul (complete reliance) combined with intentional effort.

"Your faith is proven by your effort, not your perfect outcome."

The Mindset Shift:

  • Amanah: Recognize your time, health, and youth as sacred trusts.
  • Ihsaan: Striving with excellence in the present moment, not just the finish line.
  • Halal Leisure: Rest is not a waste; it is a tool to make the soul stronger for the truth.

Lessons from the Seerah

Ka’b ibn Malik (RA)

The profound danger of "just one more day." His story teaches us the weight of procrastination in the face of duty, but more importantly, the power of sincere return and action.

Post-Uhud Resilience

The Prophet ﷺ and the Sahaba faced extreme emotional and physical fatigue. Their ability to move forward despite trauma was rooted in Tawakkul and a collective sense of purpose.

"I give my soul some relaxation with permissible enjoyment, so that it may be stronger upon the truth." — Abu Darda (RA)

Practical Action

Move from a state of delay to one of action with these "Micro-habits":

  • The 5-Minute Rule: Commit for 5 minutes just to build momentum.
  • The Life Audit: Write down 3 habits causing your biggest blocks.
  • Salah-Centric Planning: Schedule deep work blocks centering around prayer times.
  • IF-THEN Planning: "If I have my coffee, then I start the task."
  • Digital Fasting: Eliminate one specific distraction for a week as a spiritual exercise.
Reflective Moment: Always start with Bismillah. Seek refuge from laziness daily using the Prophetic Du'a.

PRACTICAL TOOLKIT

Overcoming Procrastination

The Spiritual Diagnosis

Procrastination is often more than just a time management issue; it is a spiritual hurdle. It is a tactic used to distance us from our purpose and the excellence (Ihsaan) we are called to. Overcoming it requires a shift in how we view Time as an Amanah (Trust).

Leader Execution Tools

Audit Tool

The Life Audit

Write down the one habit causing your biggest procrastination block. Address the smallest possible component of that task today to break the cycle.

Action Tool

The 5-Minute Rule

Commit to any task for just five minutes. This small window is often enough to break the mental barrier of starting and build sustained momentum.

Sacred Trust

Time as Amanah

Recognise every hour as a sacred trust. Identify your top three daily "Barakah-killers" (distractions) and eliminate one for a full week.

Structure

Salah Stacking

Structure your most demanding tasks around your Salah times. Use these natural breaks as spiritual resets to refocus your intentions.

Mindset Shift

Ihsaan vs Perfection

Perfectionism leads to paralysis. Reframe excellence as the quality of your effort in the current moment, rather than the perfect final result.

Priority Tool

Glass vs Plastic

Identify "Glass Balls" (Salah, Health, Family) that cannot be dropped, and "Plastic Balls" (Minor errands) that can bounce or wait if needed.
