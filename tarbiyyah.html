Toolkit & FAQ's

WHAT IS RAMADAN?

Tap to flip

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar. It is a month of fasting and one of the five pillars of Islam. It is a time of spiritual growth, marking the month of the Qur'an's divine revelation.

WHAT HAPPENS DURING RAMADAN?

Tap to flip

We wake up before dawn for a meal called Suhoor and fast and abstain from impulses to focus on reflection. At sunset, we break it at Iftar with water and dates as that is the sunnah of the Prophet (SAW). The nights involve Taraweeh (community prayers) and time spent in remembrance of Allah.

IS IT JUST NO FOOD AND WATER?

Tap to flip

No, it is a spiritual reset. Beyond food and water, we work on controlling our desires and nafs (self) - avoiding gossip and anger to connect deeper with Allah (SWT).

WHAT IF WE EAT IN FRONT OF YOU?

Tap to flip

There is actually a special reward for us when others eat in our presence:

Umm 'Umarah bint Ka'b reported: The Prophet (SAW) said "Verily, angels send blessings upon a fasting person when others eat in their presence until they finish."
— Tirmidhi 785

WHY DO YOU FAST?

Tap to flip

Fasting is about obeying Allah's command and aiming to achieve Taqwa (God-consciousness). It builds self-discipline, patience and deep empathy for less fortunate, reminding us that our strength comes from Allah (SWT).

HOW TO SUPPORT?

Tap to flip

A simple "Ramadan Mubarak" means a lot! Being mindful of our energy levels and adjusting socializing spots really helps. And of course, everyone is always welcome to join us for Iftar!

Virtues of Feeding People

“From the ‘ibadah of providing food for people stem many other acts of worship such as creating love and friendship towards those who are given the food, which is a means of entering Paradise, as the Prophet ﷺ said: “You will not enter Paradise until you truly believe, and you will not truly believe until you love one another.” (Muslim, 54).It also fosters the practice of sitting with righteous people and seeking reward by helping them to do acts of worship for which they gain strength by eating your food."
Renew and Realign Intentions
Aligning the heart and mind is the foundation of service. Remind your team that providing food is a profound act of 'ibadah.
“Whoever gives iftar to one who is fasting will have a reward like his, without that detracting from the reward of the fasting person in the slightest.” — Tirmidhi, 807
Pre-Iftar Logistics & Practical Essentials
Before the iftar dinner, ensure your team has the important things covered:
  • Plan a menu: Accommodate for GF, Vegetarian, Vegan, and Nut-free options.
  • Waste Management: Set up a clear system for food waste and recycling.
  • Space: Dedicated spot for dates and water.
  • Prayer Space Prep: Use plastic sheets to transition quickly.
Budgeting & Resource Management
Effective budgeting is an act of Amanah (trust). Ensure your resources are used efficiently to maximize the number of people served.
  • Track Expenses: Maintain a live spreadsheet.
  • Sponsorships: Partner with local businesses or alumni.
  • Portion Control: Work with caterers to balance generosity with waste reduction.
  • Bulk Purchasing: Buy dates and water in bulk early.
Financial Inclusivity
University life can be financially difficult. By providing free iftars, you support students under hardship.
“Whoever relieves a believer of some worldly distress, Allah will relieve him of some of the distress on the day of resurrection.” — Ibn Majah, 225

Physical Benefits of Fasting

Metabolic Control

Fasting improves insulin sensitivity and helps regulate blood sugar levels.

Cellular Repair

Fasting triggers autophagy, where cells "clean house" and trigger regeneration.

Heart Health

Improves cholesterol levels and lowers blood pressure.

Inflammation

Decreases markers of chronic inflammation.

Cognitive

Boosts brain function and supports mental clarity.

Gut Health

Promotes hormonal balance and digestive rest.
