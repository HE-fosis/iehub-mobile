Fundraising for Ramadan

Ramadan 2026 is approaching. Here are some initiatives to raise money for your ISoc, whether it is for free Iftar and Suhoor, or for charity.

Ramadan is when your university's Muslim community comes together. The Prophet (saw) said, 'Whoever feeds a person breaking his fast will earn the same reward as him, without anything being lessened from the reward of the fasting person.' [Tirmidhi]

  • Setting up smaller fundraising schemes is a good way to raise money while increasing the exposure of your ISoc.
  • Speak to your events team to set up some fundraising events before Ramadan starts.
  • See the Fundraising at events section for more details.

Large scale initiatives usually require more planning:

  • Identify what you want to achieve during Ramadan. Does your ISoc want to provide free Iftar and Suhoor? If so, how often do you want to provide this service? This will give you an estimate of the amount of money you need to raise for Ramadan.
  • Contact the relevant departments in your university about what you want to achieve this Ramadan—see if they will provide sponsorship. It is important to let them know how much you want to raise and how many meals you want to provide, for transparency. (Student Union, diversity/equality department and individual colleges if applicable, charity sponsors)
  • Speak to your local Muslim community/ Masjid. They may have Ramadan initiatives in place already, and could provide funding, meals and guidance for this time period.

Where To Start

A recommended timeline to ease you into the role.

Create a bank account (or contact your SU if they manage society finances).
On your ISoc's shared Drive (or equivalent) create a finances folder to keep your spreadsheets and financial documents in.
Reach out to your SU for any treasurer training that they provide.
Reach out to the previous treasurer to transfer ISoc's remaining finances. This is a good time to ask how your ISoc specifically manages its finances!
Review the grants your university offers and note the open and close dates.
Discuss with leadership the possibility of working with a sponsor. More information in the sponsorship section.
Record these transactions in a spreadsheet on your shared Drive (or equivalent).

Top Tips

Avoid getting overwhelmed with these simple habits!

Joining conversations and group chats with your events planning team will mean you won't need to find funding at the last-minute.
Termly or monthly run-downs of expenses (including receipts) on Excel or Sheets allow instant access to recent transactions.
Keep a list of grants/bursaries your university provides, with the date they open.
Having a charity sponsor your society will remove the pressure of trying to find last minute funds. See the Sponsorship section for more details.
When handling reimbursements, keep a copy of the receipt or invoice before sending the refund.

Sponsorships

Things To Consider

Choosing A Sponsor

When looking for charities to sponsor your ISoc you should consider the following:
  • Sustainability of their projects: Will the money raised by your society be used beneficially long term by the charity? What percentage of money donated goes towards the charity itself?
  • How long they have operated in this country: The charity should be suitably developed so that you know it won't dissolve in the near future. Asking other ISocs for their recommendations on which charity to work with is a good way to find a stable sponsorship.
  • Transparency: When asking the charity questions, do they share minimal information? As you are dealing with large amounts of money, transparency is key from both parties.
  • Annual reports: This should be available on their website.

Initial Contact

Reaching Out To The Sponsor

  • Initial contact: Meeting with a member of your chosen sponsor will allow you to ask in-depth questions regarding your relationship with the charity. If you have the facilities, assigning a sponsorship officer or equivalent in your ISoc means that there is one main point of contact with the sponsor.
  • When to ask for financial aid: Sponsors prefer to cover larger costs, such as charity events and Ramadan. They may prefer for smaller event costs such as weekly gatherings to be paid for by your ISoc. It's important to communicate clearly with the charity about what they are willing to cover.
Mutual Value

What the Sponsor Receives

Usually, all donations from your event will be returned to your charity sponsor. As before, it is important to clarify this with the sponsor.
  • Brand Awareness: Crediting the charity on social media when they are funding the event is also expected.
Collaboration

Event Expectations From The Sponsor

This depends on the charity you choose to partner with. Usually, the charity allows your society to have full control of what events you choose to host.
  • Speakers: They may present speakers who collaborate with their charity for talks which can provide an insightful opportunity for your society!

Payments & Card Machines

  • The Student Union: However, they take a small fee, so coordinate with your SU beforehand and consider whether you are raising money for your ISoc or for charity.

What Tools And Apps Are Useful?

Spreadsheets to track finances and reimbursements.
  • Google Sheets: Upskill here/ Proton
  • Excel Spreadsheets: Keep track of income, profits and losses. Check our Event Budget Template at the top of the page.
  • Google Forms, Typeform or direct messaging for committee reimbursements: For any reimbursement to go through, an invoice or receipt must be provided beforehand!
  • Collecting card payments: SumUp and SquareSpace. See Fundraising at Events on advice for handling online payments.
  • Selling tickets for events: Eventbrite, Luma or your Student Union.

how can i recieve an income

  • Membership Fees: Incentivise with perks and future discounts.
  • University Grants: Keep a list of potential grants and their open/ close dates.
  • Retail and Hospitality Discounts: Restaurants, takeaways, dessert shops, clothes shops, halal butchers. Use the Discount Card Script as a template for the contract.
  • Jumu'ah donations: Take cash and card donations, explore potential for standing orders.
  • Dowah/ Donation Stalls: Increase the visibility of ISoc in your local community whilst collecting donations.
  • Merchandise: Making merchandise available only to those who are registered members of ISoc is a good way to quantify an average number of active members.
  • Alumni Contacts: Start and maintain your Alumni network! From lecturers to members of the local community.
  • Event Ticket Sales: Reduce cost for max profit- use early bird pricing, group tickets and competitions.

What Should I Coordinate With My SU?

  • Check with your SU if either a bank account monitored by the SU or a personal bank account is better for your ISoc. Your university may have specific guidelines on where transactions must take place, so clarifying this with your SU in the beginning removes any uncertainty.
  • As the SU deals with a large number of societies, it is important to give them time to respond to reimbursements or queries. Work around bank holidays or closures when planning events in advance. The Budget Tracker Template at the top of the Finances page is a good way to keep on top of this!

How Does The SU Benefit My ISoc?

  • With an account run by the SU, it means they know exactly where your transactions are going, which can benefit you when asking for grants/ bursaries.
  • The SU account can provide run downs of your expenses instantaneously, so that you have instant access to the amount in the account without having to be responsible for the account itself.
